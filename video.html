ShopRight – Brand Ambassador Keynote Script
Full presentation script: cold open → narrative arc → demo beats → objections → vision → call‑to‑action
Jump to Teleprompter Script

1) Cold Open Hooks (choose 1)

  • “If you’re still closing sales in DMs, you’re leaking money. Let’s fix that in minutes.”
  • “What if your store could be up before this keynote ends?”
  • “Trust is a checkout page away. Today I’ll show you how sellers go from chat to cha‑ching.”

2) Introduction

Stage Direction: Confident stance, one beat pause after the hook. Smile; connect eyes to the audience left → center → right.

Script: “Hello everyone, I’m thrilled to be here. My name is [Your Name], and I represent ShopRight — the simplest way to turn your DMs into orders and your products into a high‑converting storefront. We built ShopRight for modern sellers: the Instagram brand, the WhatsApp entrepreneur, the local boutique, the digital creator. If you can take a photo of your product, you can have a store by the end of today.”

3) The Problem (Make it felt)

B‑roll: Screens of scattered chats, spreadsheets, failed follow‑ups, buyer confusion. Subtle background music builds.
  • Sales trapped in DMs → lost trust, lost time, lost orders.
  • Fragmented tools: links everywhere, no analytics, shaky delivery and payment flow.
  • Buyers want speed and certainty — “Is it available? How do I pay? When will it arrive?”

Script: “Most sellers don’t have a tech team. They have ambition. But ambition needs an engine. DMs are great for conversation. They’re terrible at conversion.”

4) The Promise

On screen: Hero line, big type. “Launch a store in minutes. Payments, delivery, analytics — all in one.”

Script: “ShopRight puts everything in one place: product pages, coupons, pay‑on‑delivery and gateways, simple delivery regions, and real analytics. You don’t need to be technical. You just need the desire to grow.”

No‑code setup Pay on Delivery + Gateways Coupons Inventory Email receipts Analytics Chatbot

5) Story: “Andrea’s First 48 Hours”

B‑roll: Quick montage — add product, set price, enable COD, share link, first order notification.

Script: “Andrea sells handmade skincare. She lives in her DMs. In 48 hours, we helped her set up ShopRight. She added five products, switched on Pay on Delivery, and shared a link in her bio and WhatsApp status. She didn’t change who she is. She changed where the checkout happens. The first order came the same day. The second came while she was asleep. That’s the power of a real store.”

6) Live Demo Beats (Narrated)

On stage: Short, paced demo — no typing walls of text. Emphasize speed and clarity.
  1. “Create product → add price → upload image → save.”
  2. “Toggle Pay on Delivery and online payment.”
  3. “Set delivery regions and fees.”
  4. “Create a coupon: WELCOME10.”
  5. “Place a test order → email receipt.”
  6. “Open dashboard analytics: earnings, orders, best time to sell.”

Narration: “If you can use your phone, you can use ShopRight. This is not software that fights you. This is software that works with you.”

7) Product Pillars → Features

Speed → Launch today

  • No‑code onboarding; we’ll do concierge setup for first‑time sellers.
  • Image upload straight from your device; gallery and variants supported.

Trust → Close the sale

  • Checkout page for credibility and clarity.
  • Pay on Delivery + gateway options, email receipts.

Revenue → Increase AOV

  • Coupons and promotions; limited‑time codes.
  • Inventory and product visibility to reduce stockouts.

Insight → Grow smarter

  • Sales over time, best hours, regional performance.
  • Cart and wishlist signals to drive remarketing.

8) Why ShopRight vs. Alternatives

  • Marketplace fees and rules you don’t control vs. your own storefront and data.
  • “Link‑in‑bio” pages with no real checkout vs. a real, auditable order flow.
  • Complicated enterprise tools vs. a seller‑first, no‑code experience.

Script: “You don’t need bloat. You need clarity. ShopRight is opinionated on your behalf: fewer clicks, faster setup, better outcomes.”

9) The Offer

Slide: “4‑Day Rapid Trial + Free Concierge Setup” + big CTA.

Script: “Today, everyone here gets a four‑day rapid trial. We’ll even help you import your first products and set up payments. Your job is to share your link and watch what happens.”

CTAs (choose based on venue): “Scan the QR on screen”, “Visit: yourstore.link/start”, “DM ‘START’ and we’ll set you up today”.

10) Objections, Pre‑empted

  • “I’m too busy.” → “Send 5 product photos and prices — we’ll build it for you today.”
  • “I’m not technical.” → “No code. We set it up. You keep selling.”
  • “My buyers want to chat.” → “Keep chatting. When they’re ready, a checkout link is one tap away.”
  • “Payments are complicated.” → “Start with Pay on Delivery; add gateways like Paystack/Flutterwave later.”
  • “Can I move from DMs/spreadsheets?” → “Yes. We import and you go live with what you already have.”

11) Vision

Script: “Our vision is simple: every seller deserves a beautiful, trustworthy storefront, without a technical cofounder. We’re building the rails for the next million merchants — local, global, digital, physical. Wherever commerce happens, we’ll make checkout effortless.”

12) Closing (Finalization)

Pause. Softer tone, then strong final line. Gesture to screen/QR. Invite applause.

Script: “You’ve done the hard part — creating products people love. Let ShopRight do the easy part — creating a store people trust. Start your free setup now. I can’t wait to celebrate your first order — and your next hundred.”

Final line: “From chat to checkout, from hustle to growth — that’s ShopRight. Thank you.”

Speaker Notes (Delivery Tips)

  • Slow down on the offer; make eye contact; gesture to CTA.
  • Use stories instead of specs; specs support stories.
  • Keep the demo moving; show outcomes, not menus.
  • Invite action in the room — QR scan, chat reply, DM keyword.
  • End on a confident, memorable line.

This keynote script is built to stand out: clear problem framing, human story, minimal friction demo, bold offer, and a resonant close. Use the teleprompter section verbatim or adapt with your voice and venue‑specific CTAs.
